[Strophe.

If I, O Kithairon, some vision can borrow

From seercraft, if still there is wit in the old, Long, long, through the deep-orbèd Moon of the morrow—

So hear me, Olympus!—thy tale shall be told. O mountain of Thebes, a new Theban shall praise thee,

One born of thy bosom, one nursed at thy springs; And the old men shall dance to thy glory, and raise thee

To worship, O bearer of joy to my kings. And thou, we pray, Look down in peace, O Apollo; I-ê, I-ê!
