JOCASTA.

What should man do with fear, who hath but Chance Above him, and no sight nor governance

![Sidenote: vv. 979-993]

Of things to be? To live as life may run, No fear, no fret, were wisest 'neath the sun. And thou, fear not thy mother. Prophets deem A deed wrought that is wrought but in a dream. And he to whom these things are nothing, best Will bear his burden.

OEDIPUS.

All thou counsellest Were good, save that my mother liveth still. And, though thy words be wise, for good or ill Her I still fear.

JOCASTA.

Think of thy father's tomb! Like light across our darkness it hath come.

OEDIPUS.

Great light; but while she lives I fly from her.
